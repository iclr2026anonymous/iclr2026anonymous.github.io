Framework Overview



Abstract

Exoskeleton research shows promise for mobility enhancement, but providing appropriate assistance remains challenging due to complex human adaptation to assistive forces. To address this, current techniques require extensive experiments where participants must walk for hours, creating a paradox: those who most need assistance often cannot even handle daily life activities, making participation in these demanding procedures impossible. We present Exo-plore, a simulation framework combining musculoskeletal simulation and deep reinforcement learning to optimize hip exoskeleton assistance without human trials. Exo-plore is capable of: (1) generating realistic gait data that accurately captures human adaptation to assistive forces, (2) producing reliable optimization results despite inherent stochasticity in gait patterns, and (3) generalizing to pathological gaits, which showed high linear correlations between pathology severity and optimal assistance. By replacing demanding human trials with scalable simulation, Exo-plore enables personalized exoskeleton controller development for both able-bodied and mobility-impaired individuals who cannot participate in traditional optimization procedures.

Results

Gait Generation (Unassisted)

As a useful sanity check, Exo-plore generates realistic human gait patterns without assistive forces, with overall joint kinematics and variability closely resembling those of healthy walking.

Gait Generation (Assisted)

Exo-plore captures realistic hip kinematics and assistive dynamics across walking speeds, validating its effectiveness through consistent trends in both motion and power outputs.

Optimization Results

We demonstrate the applicability of Exo-plore by discovering and analyzing optimal exoskeleton control parameters for both able-bodied and disabled individuals.

Generalization to the pathologic gait

We evaluate Exo-plore across five musculoskeletal disorder types, revealing clear links between optimal exoskeleton assistance and the severity of each condition.
